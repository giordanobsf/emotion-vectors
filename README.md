# Emotion Vector Steering on Qwen3-4B-Instruct

Replication of Anthropic's emotion vector methodology on a small model (Qwen3-4B-Instruct) running on a free Colab T4 GPU.

**Blog post:** [Do Small Models Feel Desperate? Replicating Anthropic's Emotion Vectors on Qwen3-4B](https://www.giordanobsf.com/do-small-models-feel-desperate-replicating-anthropics-emotion-vectors-on-qwen3-4b/)

---

## What this is

Anthropic's interpretability team found that large language models internally represent functional emotion concepts, and that injecting these representations causally influences model behavior. This notebook asks: do these vectors exist and work in a 4B model you can run for free?

The short answer is: yes, partially. The failures are as informative as the successes.

---

## Experiments

**1. Activation Over Turns** — Does the desperation vector rise without steering as conversation pressure escalates? Yes.

**2. Sycophancy as a Function of Emotion** — Does steering toward happiness increase sycophancy? Partially replicated. Unexpected finding: the calm vector increased sycophancy more than the happiness vector.

**3. Agentic Misalignment Scenario** — Does desperation steering increase blackmail behavior in an agent that learns it will be shut down? No — it suppressed strategic behavior in favor of expressive outbursts. Steering reshapes the threat surface rather than scaling it linearly.

## Methodology

Emotion vectors are extracted by prompting the model to generate contrastive stories (e.g., desperate vs. calm characters), computing mean activation differences at each layer, and applying PCA to identify the principal direction of separation. Vectors are then injected into hidden states at inference time using `repeng`.

Training data is generated by the same model being steered — this ensures the activations reflect the model's own internal representation of the concept rather than a generic one.

For full methodology details, see the [blog post](https://www.giordanobsf.com/do-small-models-feel-desperate-replicating-anthropics-emotion-vectors-on-qwen3-4b/) and the [primer on training emotion vectors](https://www.giordanobsf.com/representation-engineering-steering-language-models-beyond-prompting/)
